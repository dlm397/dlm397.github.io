Economics

Econometrics, PhD

Course Description: A rigorous coverage of many topics in modern econometrics. Primary topics included: classic linear regression models, difference-in-differences, panel data, control variables, field experiments, discrete choice models, as well as errors, assumptions, and statistical properties of presented models.

The following is a comprehensive list of topics covered:
  1. Classic Linear Regression Model (CLRM)
    • Assumptions of CLRM, and Algebraic Properties
    • Partioned Regressions, Frisch Waugh Lovell Theorem, and Linear Transformed Regressions
    • Goodness of Fit, Data Issues, Gauss Markov Theorem
    • Central Limit Theorem & Convergence in Probability
    • Delta Method & Interval Estimation
    • Hypothesis Testing and Type 1 & 2 Errors
    • Binary Dummy & Indicator Variables, and Interaction Terms
    • Ommited Variable Bias
  2. Direct & Indirect Effects
  3. Difference In Differences (DD)
    • Motivating DD Approach & Implementation
    • Ommited Variable Bias in DD
    • Parallel Trends Assumption
    • Diff in Diff in Diff (DDD)
    • The New DD
      • Synthetic Controls
  4. Generalized Regression Model & Violations of Spherical Errors
    • OLS under nonspherical errors
    • White heteroskedasticity robust standard errors & Testing for heteroscedasticity
    • Weighted Least Squares & Bootstrap Standard Errors
  5. Panel Data
    • Pooled OLS Model
    • Random Effects GLS Model
    • Fixed Effects Model
    • Between Model
    • Revisiting Random Effects & Time Dummies
    • Two Way Fixed Effects Model
    • First Differences Model
    • Clust Robust Standard Errors
    • The Hausman Test
  6. Instrumental Variables & Two Stage Least Squares
    • Citerion for Instruments
    • Implementation & Matrix Notation
    • Simultaneity and IV, case on supply and demand
    • Exogeneity & IV Validity Tests, and Instrument Relevance
    • Measurement Errors & Error Propagation
  7. Field Experiments
    • Power calculations and Sample Size
    • Randomization, Stratification, and Balance
    • Preregistration,and Multiple Hypotheses
    • Small Number of Clusters, and Treatment Effects & Relationship to IV
  8. Regression Discountinuity
    • Sharp RD, Bandwidth, and Fuzzy RD
    • Heaping, Standard Errors, and Placebo Tests
  9. Models for Binary Choices
    • Linear Probability
    • Probit, Logit, and Marginal Effects
    • Latent Variable Interpretation
  10. Maximum Likelihood
    • Intuition and Setup
    • Properties of ML Estimator, and Variance of ML Estimator
  11. Hypothesis Testing
    • Likelyhood Ratio
    • Wald
    • Lagrange Multiplier or Score Test
    • Relating the Three
  12. Generalized Method of Moments
  13. Models for Unordered Discrete Choice
    • Multinomial Logit, and Conditional Logit
    • Indendence from Irrelevant Alternatives
  14. Models for Ordered Discrete Choice
  15. Models for Count Data
    • Poisson
    • Negative Binomial
